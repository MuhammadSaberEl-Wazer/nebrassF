That you may become righteous

Being pious is one of the most important thing when it comes to fasting. Fasting does a lot of physical processes to the body as it decreaces the desire of the stomach and mutes the sexual appetite. Whoever fasts in a regular basis would overcome these two desires more easily. Strengthening willpower and acquiring patience.
We have to fear Allah and obey His orders throughout this month. The Prophet mohamed salah allah alih wslam has called Ramadan the month of patience as it increases Muslim's will power to control his desire and the selfishness of his soul. This kind of practice allows him to be strong in following Allah's orders.
'He has left his food, drink and desires for My sake. The fast is for Me. So I will reward (the fasting person) for it and the reward of good deeds is multiplied ten times'" (Bukhari).
it has been related that the prophet said, "Ameen, ameen, ameen." to "Jibrael (peace be upon him),once he came to the prophet and said, "Whoever witnesses the month of Ramadan and is not forgiven, he enters Jahanam, may Allah cast him away. Say Ameen." So I said Ameen."

The month of Ramadan is a chance to make up for the time lost around the year in other work and revert to Allah, beg forgiveness for the sins done around the year, and finally, a chance to better the life waiting in the Hereafter. It offers every Muslim an opportunity to strengthen his Emaan, purify his heart and soul, and to remove the evil effects of the sins committed by him.
All those who have performed their spiritual duties towards the Creator and the created and have accomplished the mission ordained upon them in this month, have got every right to celebrate Eid-ul-Fitr, as a day of successful culmination, but of course, not before paying Fitra, a simple obligatory gesture of kindness towards the poor, to enable them to enjoy the holy day.
Children should be encouraged to start fasting at an early age to learn how to stay strong in such difficult times.
There are some wrong deeds made during the day in Ramadan:

Spending too much: Ramadan has to be the time to avoid being wasteful, not only that but also learning to be fully prepared in facing harder times and abstacles. the thing is that, many people that i know even go beyond their limits to spend so much in Ramadan, whether in the varieties of food they eat, or by the other types of spending, in my openion they need to know the difference beween the things that things they need and the things they want.
Staying awake during the night and sleeping during the day: Meditation for soul upliftment can be practiced in the month of Ramadan with determination. Some people sleep during most of the day when they are fasting or else engage themselves in activities. Ramadan is not a time of being lazy, also what is the point of Fasting, we are fasting to feel as a poor obressed person feels, we're putting our shoes in a less fortunate situation so we can feel more empathy for him and that is how you offer these poor people help because you know how suffering and hardships they go through. In addition, some may spend a great portion of the night indulging in eating and drinking and socializing.
Reading the Quran too fast: Some insist on finishing the whole Quran once or more, even if they have to read very fast. Although reading the Quran many times is desirable, this should not be done hastily, especially during the Tarawih prayer without pondering upon its meaning. And if we think about it we should not read it as we read an article we have to feel it and to get it's meaning so Allah gives us the reward
Eating too much: This month gives us a message to realize about the poor and needy people. Filling appetite more than required is not the mandate of Ramadan, also it is not really healthy.There are few people who eat too much in the month of Ramadan. It is been observed that the people eat more in the month of Ramadan than in any other month. But Allah is only pleased with those, who eat less and charity more in this month.
